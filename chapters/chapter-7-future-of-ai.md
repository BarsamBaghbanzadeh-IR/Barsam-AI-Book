# 🔮 Chapter 7: The Future I See  
**And You Can Be Part of It Too**

---

> ✨ Sometimes, late at night, when I'm studying or just thinking, I wonder what the world will look like in 20 years.  
> Not out of fear — but out of curiosity.  
> I'm Barsam. 14 years old. From Rasht.  
> And I wrote this chapter not as a student, but as a teenager who is eager to build.

---

## 🌱 1. Why I Think About the Future

Maybe it sounds simple, but every day I see things that make me stop and think.  
A phone that unlocks with a glance.  
An app that knows the way better than I do.  
A chatbot that can talk to me for hours without getting tired.

These are not just tools.  
They are signs of a new world — a world being built right now, not in movies, but in labs, in the minds of scientists, and even in our own homes.

> 💭 I think about the future because I know that the future, no matter how far it seems, is built from the decisions we make today.

---

## 🌍 2. What Will Tomorrow Look Like?

Let me share a few things I see on the horizon — not as predictions, but as the view of a teenager looking ahead:

| 🌟 Change | 💡 How It Will Affect Our Lives |
|-----------|--------------------------------|
| 🧠 **AI Becomes Our Constant Companion** | No more typing or clicking. Just thinking, speaking, or even gesturing. Machines will understand us — not the other way around. |
| 🌍 **Digital Justice Takes Shape** | Smart tools will be available to everyone. A student in a remote village will have the same access to education and healthcare as a scientist in the capital. |
| 💡 **Creativity Becomes More Important Than Memory** | Machines will remember everything. But humans will create meaning — they will tell stories, connect emotionally, and imagine new possibilities. |

> 🚀 This is just a glimpse of a much larger picture. The future is bigger and more amazing than any table can contain.

---

## 🔥 3. What Is My Role in This Future?

The truth is, I — Barsam — don't want to be just a spectator.  
I don't want to be only a consumer of what others build.

I want to understand.  
I want to learn.  
I want to make mistakes and get back up.

Maybe one day I won't solve every problem.  
But I can be one of those who helps solve them.

> 💪 And to me, that is the greatest power any teenager can have:  
> **Knowing that you can make a difference — even if you start with just one small step.**

---

## 📖 4. This Book Is More Than Just a Book

This book is a reminder.  
To myself, to you, to anyone who reads it:

> ✨ **We can.**  
> **We know.**  
> **We are responsible.**

I wrote this book not to say I know everything,  
but to say:  
*"I started just like you — from a small point, with a simple question."*

---

## 🌟 5. The Future Starts Today

You might not believe it, but right now, at this very moment,  
by reading this text, you are taking one step closer to the future.

So if you ever thought you were too young, too small, or that it's too late,  
let me tell you:

**No.**  
The time is now.  
The place is here.  
And you are powerful enough to begin.

> ⏳ The future waits for no one. But it smiles at those who move toward it.

---

## ✍️ 6. A Final Word from Me, Barsam

> 🌱 *"You cannot predict the future,*  
> *but you can build it,*  
> *with small hands, an open mind, and a brave heart."*

I wrote this line myself.  
Not out of pride —  
but out of a belief that grows stronger every day.

And if you share that belief,  
then we are walking the same path.
