# Chapter 1: What is Artificial Intelligence?

> Artificial Intelligence means "machines that can think and solve problems like humans." But this definition is just the tip of the iceberg.

**The Birth of an Idea:**
In 1956, at a summer conference at Dartmouth College in the United States, a group of scientists used the word **"Artificial Intelligence"** for the first time. They believed that "every aspect of learning or any other feature of intelligence can in principle be so precisely described that a machine can be made to simulate it."

From that day to today, AI has come a long way. From simple programs that only did math, to systems that can write poetry, paint pictures, and even have conversations with you.

**How Does AI Work?**
AI is like a child. You show it thousands of pictures, and it learns to tell a cat from a dog. You give it millions of texts, and it learns to write like a human.

This process is called **"Machine Learning"**. Machines find patterns in data and make predictions.

**Three Types of AI:**
1. **Narrow AI:** Like your phone's voice assistant — it can only do specific tasks.
2. **General AI:** A machine that can do anything a human can do (not built yet).
3. **Super AI:** Smarter than the smartest humans in history (just an idea for now).

**Where is AI Today?**
Today, AI is in your phone, in your car, in hospitals, in schools, and even in agriculture. In less than 100 years, this technology has gone from an idea to an inseparable part of our lives.

And this is just the beginning...
