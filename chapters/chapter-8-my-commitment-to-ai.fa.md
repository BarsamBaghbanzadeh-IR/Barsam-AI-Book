# 🌾 Chapter 8: Me, Barsam, and an Ocean of Glory Flowing in My Veins

---

> *"Human beings are members of a whole, for in creation they are of one essence."*
> — **Saadi**

Every time I read this poem, something awakens inside me. I remember that I am not just a teenager from Rasht. I am the heir to a civilization that gave the world humanity, science, and beauty. And that is my greatest motivation to build the future.

---

## 🏛️ When a King Wrote the First Charter of Human Rights

2,500 years ago, in 538 BC, Cyrus the Great ordered his message to be written on a baked clay cylinder. When I read this text, I see how a great king spoke of justice in his own words:

> *"I am Cyrus, king of the world, great king, righteous king, king of Babylon, king of Sumer and Akkad, king of the four corners of the world."*
> 
> *"When I entered Babylon without battle and fighting, all the people received me with joy."*
> 
> *"I ordered that all the temples that had been closed should be opened. I returned all the gods of these temples to their places. I brought back all the people who had been scattered and displaced to their homes, and I rebuilt their ruined houses."*

And then he declared:

> *"I announce that everyone is free to worship any religion they desire and to reside in any place they wish, provided that they do not usurp the rights of others."*

Can you believe that more than two thousand years ago, in this land, equality, religious freedom, and justice were the law of an emperor? This is not a myth — it is a truth baked into the clay of history.

---

## ⚜️ The World's First Coins; When Darius Organized the Global Economy

But Iran was not only a pioneer in ethics and politics. Darius the Great, the Achaemenid king, around 515 BC, ordered the minting of the world's first gold and silver coins. These gold coins were called "Daric" — a coin that was 98% pure gold.

Minting this coin was not just an economic measure. The Daric became the common language of power in the ancient world; a symbol that carried the authority of the empire to the farthest borders. Even the Greek enemies of Iran were forced to accept this coin. And no satrapy had the right to mint coins without Darius's permission — this indicates a centralized and powerful system.

---

## 🔬 Scientists Who Changed the World

If we talk about computer algorithms today, we must know that their roots go back to Khwarizmi, the great Iranian scientist. He founded the science of algebra, and his name lives on forever in the word "algorithm."

Abu Rayhan Biruni theorized about the Earth's rotation on its axis more than a thousand years ago. Avicenna was a model for world medicine for centuries with his book "The Canon." And Abu Bakr al-Razi taught the world new methods of treatment.

All of these were Iranians. In an era when many parts of the world were in darkness, this land was the torchbearer of science and wisdom.

---

## 🎨 Poets Who Became the World's Language

Ferdowsi, who revived the Persian language and identity with his "Shahnameh." Hafiz, whose every ghazal is a window into the world of meaning. Saadi, whose poem "Human beings are members of a whole..." is now installed at the United Nations. And Rumi, who is one of the best-selling poets in the West.

Aren't these reasons for pride? Doesn't remembering all this glory give you new energy when you feel disappointed?

---

## ⚙️ Amazing Inventions That Emerged from This Land

The world's first refrigerators were built by ancient Iranians. The Qanat, an engineering masterpiece for extracting water from the heart of the desert, is an Iranian innovation that spread throughout the world. Windcatchers, windmills, the postal service, the alphabet, backgammon, and even sulfuric acid — all of these were either invented by Iranians or first emerged in this land.

Iranian civilization, 400 years BC, had perfected the technique of making refrigerators.

---

## 🌟 Final Word; From the Heart of History to My Commitment

When I think of all this greatness, my heart fills with hope. I understand that disappointment has no place in my culture. When my ancestors were able to write the first charter of human rights, mint the first coins, gift the science of algebra and algorithms to the world, and build refrigerators in the heart of the desert, then I can too.

**My commitment to the future is born from this glorious past.**

I will learn, I will build, and I will never give up. Because I know that I am the heir to a civilization that gave meaning to the world, to humanity, and to science.

> *"Powerful is the one who is wise, from knowledge the old heart becomes young."* — **Ferdowsi**

**🇮🇷 From Rasht, with love for Iran, pride in the past, and hope for the future.**
